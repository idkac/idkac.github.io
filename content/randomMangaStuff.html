Manga 3: Houkago Kitaku Biyori

Here is the synopsis:

Shun was invited to the Going-Home Club by Naoki Satou, also known as "Chokki-chan". Despite refusing, he ends up being somewhat forcefully involved in the club's activities! A Going Home Club rom-com featuring a detour-loving high school girl and a new high school student who has lost his dream.

So this one's gonna be pretty short. The manga is basically just a straight forward romcom slice of life, and it really doesn't do anything too special. But what I will say is that it does the basics really well, and it doesn't go out of its way to do something weird or out of the box just so the manga "different"

However, this itself is not enough for me to actually write a blog about it, so here's what really stood out in this manga for me:

Yep that's it

As you know, I have read a lot of romance mangas (and I do mean A LOT), and in basically every single one of those mangas they'll have to do an obligatory handholding scene. Most of the time it's just them like walking somewhere and the mc will awkwardly reach out his hand and boom that's it.

Manga 4: Isekai Mokushiroku Mynoghra: Hametsu no Bunmei de Hajimeru Sekai Seifuku

Based on the title length alone you can tell it's an isekai (also last one i promise)

Here's the synopsis:

Bedridden and abandoned, Takuto Ira spent his days in the hospital trying to beat the illness that was slowly killing him. His only solace lay in the nation management strategy game Eternal Nations, where he had built a reputation as the greatest player to ever live. After succumbing to his disease, Takuto awoke within the game next to Atou-the Hero unit for his favorite civilization, Mynoghra. Now they'll set out to conquer the world together (again), one unit, settlement, and level at a time!

So I've been on a binge-fest recently where I specifically read mangas from the oddly specific genre of "kingdom building isekais", and this was one of the manga's I found during this binge. I will say that this was probably the most unique concept out of all the mangas I've read in this genre, and it is quite the shame that there aren't any more mangas with a similar setting (which is kinda weird considering that rts is not even that unpopular).

The actual manga is pretty alright, and I found the overall experience quite enjoyable (although the recent chapters have been quite schizo and hard to follow). I think the way that rts was incorporated into the kingdom building theme was really interesting and was a nice twist to this genre. The inclusion of other game types and how they affect the world was also quite interesting, especially the idea of unskippable cutscenes in rpgs (although I will say that the way they introduced this was quite unnecessary and also very cringe). There is also the complaint of very very slow releases, which is also kinda cringe but it is what it is.
